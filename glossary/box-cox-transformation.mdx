## Box-Cox transformation

Transforms the distribution of non-normal data into a normal distribution.

The transformation is defined as:

$$
x^{(\lambda)} = \begin{cases}
  \frac{x^{\lambda} - 1}{\lambda} & \lambda \neq 0 \\
  \ln(x) & \lambda = 0
\end{cases}
$$

The parameter $\lambda$ can be adjusted to maximize normality.
